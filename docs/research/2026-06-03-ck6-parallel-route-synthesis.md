# 2026-06-03 CK-6 Parallel Route Synthesis

## Framing

CK-6 should be framed as a six-route methodological program, not as evidence
that a computational compound already has human, neural, therapeutic, or
biological effects. The routes are useful because they force the same question
from different angles:

> Given a baseline state and a bounded perturbation, what moved, what failed,
> what drifted off target, and what returned toward baseline?

Spencer discussion concepts belong here as motivation only. The raw EEG bridge
motivates direct stimulus-response provenance instead of black-box proxy
claims. Drosophila motivates a tractable perturbation substrate with explicit
target/off-target/washout readouts. Multi-omics and virtual-cell work motivate
layered transition records and held-out perturbation discipline. None of these
analogies proves that CK recipes are biological, prosocial, therapeutic, or
ketamine-like.

## Six Routes

| Route | Current role | Connected output | Claim boundary |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1. CK scheduled LLM assay | Run bounded activation-cocktail recipes with site, timing, dose, gates, and proxy guardrails. | CK-4 Modal reports and transition records for prompt-matched baseline-to-perturbation comparisons. | One small open-model steering assay; not a robust behavioral, human, neural, or drug-effect result. |
| 2. Transition records | Normalize CK and toy-substrate runs into lane-agnostic perturbation records. | Shared fields for baseline state, perturbation, dose, site, timing, observed transition, side effects, antagonist checks, washout, and replication context. | Bookkeeping discipline, not evidence that substrates are equivalent. |
| 3. Raw EEG/fMRI bridge | Build auditable manifests before any brain-response encoder is trusted. | THINGS-EEG2-first stimulus/feature/response provenance rows with visual, semantic, affective, and weak social-scene controls. | Representation-learning infrastructure only; no social-cohesion, synchrony, or intervention claim. |
| 4. Drosophila toy substrate | Test perturbation grammar outside text on a small graph/dynamics fixture. | Transmitter-class edge-scaling matrix with target movement, off-target movement, instability, selectivity, and washout. | Synthetic toy graph readout; not fly behavior, pharmacology, organism biology, or neural validation. |
| 5. SAE and feature-readiness route | Keep sparse-feature inspection matched to compatible models, layers, and hooks. | Environment/readiness notes plus future feature reports that preserve signed direction, model match, and token/example evidence. | No named SAE feature should be treated as a cohesion feature without matched evidence and hard-negative transfer. |
| 6. Paper synthesis route | Tie the routes into a falsifiable research narrative without collapsing them into one claim. | A claim-limited paper frame: compute assays first, provenance second, biological/human validation later. | Synthesis is an argument structure, not an empirical result. |

## Proven Now

- CK-4 scheduled recipes can execute on Modal for
  `Qwen/Qwen2.5-0.5B-Instruct`, producing structured reports and 30 transition
  records from six held-out prompts.
- The first CK-4 result is weak and mixed: proxy guardrails-only slightly beats
  scheduled CK-1 recipes, and CK-1-only schedules show no mean movement on the
  small prompt set.
- Transition records can represent both CK-style scheduled cocktail reports and
  toy Drosophila substrate sweeps without changing their source exporters.
- The raw EEG route now has a THINGS-EEG2-style manifest-builder shape that
  treats paths as provenance metadata and keeps restricted data out of git.
- The Drosophila route has a synthetic graph fixture and transmitter matrix
  format that reports target, off-target, instability, selectivity, and washout
  fields.
- The virtual-cell/multi-omics route has supplied the right evaluation grammar:
  baseline state, perturbation, observed transition, side effects, held-out
  contexts, naive baselines, and washout.

## Not Proven

- No CK recipe has shown a robust prosocial effect. The current scheduled run is
  too small, proxy-controlled, and mixed for that claim.
- No human behavioral effect has been tested. Cooperation, trust, bonding,
  empathy, and social cohesion remain unvalidated.
- No human neural effect has been tested. EEG/fMRI documents are provenance and
  encoder scaffolds, not evidence of neural synchrony or a social signature.
- No biological or pharmacological effect has been shown. Drosophila and
  ketamine/HNK language are design analogies, not drug or organism claims.
- No SAE feature has been named as a stable cohesion feature. Sparse-feature
  claims need matched model/hook evidence, signed-pole preservation, token-level
  review, and hard-negative transfer.
- The routes do not prove each other. A toy graph washout result, an LLM
  transition record, and a future EEG encoder would remain separate evidence
  types until a specific validation study connects them.

## Next Falsification Gate

The next gate should be framed as a set of ways for the program to fail cleanly:

1. CK scheduled LLM assay: a larger held-out prompt set with durable per-axis
   guardrail vectors must show a scheduled CK component beating baseline and
   guardrails-only while side effects stay flat.
2. Transition records: every promoted run must export baseline-matched records
   with complete dose, site, timing, side-effect, and washout fields; missing
   provenance blocks promotion.
3. Raw EEG/fMRI bridge: a THINGS-EEG2 encoder must beat shuffled controls with
   visual/semantic baselines before weak social-scene features are interpreted,
   and those features must be residualized against visual, semantic, and
   affective controls.
4. Drosophila substrate: the toy matrix must reject broad claims and report
   failures such as off-target movement, instability, or washout residue as
   first-class outcomes.
5. SAE route: any candidate feature must survive matched model/layer/hook
   inspection, signed projection checks, token/example review, and held-out
   pseudo-cohesion fault classes before it is named.
6. Paper synthesis: the paper frame must keep compute-only assays,
   representation-learning bridges, toy-substrate measurements, and future
   human/neural validation in separate evidence buckets.

## Paper Claim Shape

Allowed near-term claim:

> CK-6 organizes computational-compound work as a falsifiable perturbation
> program: scheduled LLM assays, transition records, raw EEG/fMRI provenance,
> toy circuit substrates, SAE-readiness checks, and paper synthesis now share a
> state-transition vocabulary while preserving separate validation standards.

Disallowed stronger claim:

> CK recipes improve real human social cohesion, induce neural synchrony,
> emulate ketamine, identify a biological mechanism, or produce therapeutic
> effects.

The synthesis should keep the ambition visible while making the evidence level
boringly explicit. That is the most defensible paper posture right now.
